In-Mold Labeling (IML) Solutions

An innovative, single-step labeling process enhancing aesthetics, durability, and efficiency.

The IML Revolution: Elevating Product Packaging

In-mold labeling is a groundbreaking technique that integrates the labeling and molding processes into a single, efficient step. This innovation transforms product packaging, offering unparalleled design freedom, superior durability, and significant production efficiencies compared to traditional post-mold labeling methods.

What is In-Mold Labeling (IML)?

IML involves placing a pre-printed polypropylene (PP) label into a mold. When the molten plastic is injected, it fuses with the label, making the label an integral part of the final product. Unlike pressure-sensitive labels that are applied after molding, an IML label is seamless, permanent, and cannot be peeled off. This creates a "no-label" look with premium, high-resolution graphics that cover the entire container.

The 3 Core In-Mold Labeling Methods

Injection Molding IML

The most common method, ideal for rigid containers like food packaging, lids, and automotive parts. Offers high precision and excellent graphic quality.

Blow Molding IML

Used for hollow products like bottles and canisters. The label is placed in the mold before the plastic is blown to fit the mold's shape.

Thermoforming IML

Suitable for cups, trays, and tubs. A plastic sheet is heated and formed over a mold where the label has been pre-placed.

The 4 Major Advantages of IML

Enhanced Brand Awareness

High-resolution, full-wrap graphics create a premium look that makes your product stand out on the shelf.

Cost & Efficiency

Combines molding and labeling into one step, reducing production time, labor, and storage costs.

Superior Durability

Labels are scratch-resistant, waterproof, and won't peel or fade, even in harsh conditions.

Environmental Sustainability

Container and label are made from the same material, making the entire product 100% recyclable.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What design factors should I consider for IML?

Key factors include the shape and curvature of the product, gate location (where plastic is injected), and ensuring artwork accounts for potential stretching or distortion. Our experts can guide you through the process.

How thick are IML labels?

IML labels are typically very thin, ranging from 40 to 100 microns. The exact thickness depends on the application, desired finish (e.g., orange peel, glossy), and the size of the product.
