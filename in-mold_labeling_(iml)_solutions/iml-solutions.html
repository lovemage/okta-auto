What is IML?

IML involves placing a pre-printed polypropylene (PP) label into a mold. When molten plastic is injected, it fuses with the label, making it an integral part of the final product.

Unlike pressure-sensitive labels, an IML label is seamless, permanent, and creates a premium "no-label" look.

Core Methods

Injection Molding IML

The most common method, ideal for rigid containers like food packaging, lids, and automotive parts.

Blow Molding IML

Used for hollow products like bottles. The label is placed in the mold before the plastic is blown to shape.

Thermoforming IML

Suitable for cups and trays. A plastic sheet is heated and formed over a mold with a pre-placed label.
